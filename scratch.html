What is Immigration Law?

Immigration law governs who may enter, reside within, and become a citizen of a country. In the United States, immigration law is a federal matter and covers visas, green cards, asylum, and naturalization.

As an aspiring immigration attorney, I am passionate about helping immigrants navigate this complex system and find legal pathways to safety and opportunity.

Common Immigration Pathways

  1. Family-Based Immigration
    • Immediate relatives of U.S. citizens
    • Family preference categories
    • Form I-130 Petition
  2. Employment-Based Immigration
    • Employer sponsorship
    • Labor certification
    • EB-1, EB-2, EB-3 visa categories
  3. Asylum and Refugee Status
    • Form I-589 Application
    • Must prove fear of persecution
    • Based on race, religion, nationality, or political opinion
  4. Naturalization
    • Form N-400 Application
    • Must be a permanent resident for 5 years
    • Must pass civics and English tests
